Understanding Plant Variety Protection & Other Relevant Policy

  • Intellectual Property & Food Security

    Plant variety protection (PVP) is a type of intellectual property intended to provide an incentive to invest in research and development of new and improved plant varieties.

  • The Convention for the Protection of New Varieties of Plants (UPOV)

    The only international regime for the protection of plant varieities.

  • Beyond UPOV: Other Relevant Policy

    Intellectual property, including plant variety protection, is part of a complex and evolving policy landscape. The contentiousness of the new biopolitical environment is reflected in policy dialogue using language that did not exist even a few decades ago: Farmers' Rights, GMOs, biopiracy. Policy can provide a resilient enabling environment if it embraces interconnectivity strategically, and the first step is understanding the interconnections in the policy landscape.
