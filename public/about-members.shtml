Who Benefits from Plant Variety Protection?


UPOV's stated mission is:
To provide and promote an effective system of plant variety protection, with the aim of encouraging the development of new varieties of plants, for the benefit of society.

If the goal is to benefit society, it is important to understand better whom the IP system is serving and who it is not. Critical questions include:

  • Are certain segments of the population at risk of being left behind?
  • What is the potential impact intellectual property regimes have or may have on farmers’ ability to save and exchange seed?
  • What are the technology preferences implicit in IP driven agricultural policies?

Futhermore, there is a need greater clarity on the links between IP policy-making and sustainable development, in particular food security and poverty alleviation.
